Substitution zoo foothold:
- substitution extension/refinement facts for association-list bindings;
- coverage invariants for substitutions over arbitrary nested atoms/lists;
- full arbitrary-depth match_sound: returned substitutions extend the input,
  cover the pattern variables, and instantiate the pattern to the matched
  subject;
- preservation of already-bound variables across fresh binding extension;
- explicit-substitution composition facts for inner/outer binding hits;
- fuelled variable dereference facts for chained bindings and self-cycle
  cutoff witnesses;
- branch-local binding trail and rollback facts for speculative match steps;
- occurs-check binding wrapper with positive and negative variable witnesses;
- Metamath-style token/list substitution with append and empty-substitution
  facts;
- positional substitution witnesses for de Bruijn-like variable indexing;
- quote-preserving substitution witness matching the PeTTa-to-HE translator's
  source-level "do not substitute under quote" behavior.
- first concrete constraint abstraction with equality, match, and abbreviation
  constraints; append/add preservation facts; positive/negative examples; and
  a proof that successful matching satisfies the match constraint.
- constraint queue/defer/simplify facts: simplification preserves exactly the
  same obligations under a substitution, deferred enqueue preserves the queue
  obligations, and covered solved constraints remain solved under later
  substitution extensions.
- rule-producing constraint simplification: equality/match/abbreviation
  constraints can produce binding extensions plus residual constraints or an
  explicit failure; queue-level simplification extends substitutions
  monotonically and is sound when residual constraints hold.
- occurs-check-aware rule-producing simplification and solved-form loop:
  direct and post-substitution cycles are rejected, successful loop results
  extend the input substitution, and the loop has a final-substitution
  preservation theorem when residual constraints hold.
- solved-form extraction from abbreviation constraints, with membership and
  soundness facts for extracted bindings.
- first evaluator-to-substitution bridge: equality steps instantiate their
