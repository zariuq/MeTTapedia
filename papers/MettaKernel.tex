\begin{abstract}
We set out to build one small \emph{trusted} proof/inference kernel, written
\emph{in \MeTTa{}} (as an ordinary \MeTTa{} program, executed on the \CeTTa{}
engine), that can express and check proofs in \emph{both} foundations we care
about: CIC-grade \emph{dependent type theory} (the foundation of Lean and Coq)
and \emph{higher-order Tarski--Grothendieck set theory} (\HOTG{}, the classical
higher-order logic of Megalodon). The kernel is a \MeTTa{} program --- not a new
\CeTTa{} language substrate --- in the same spirit as \code{pverify}, an existing
Metamath proof checker written in \MeTTa{}. Two principles govern the work.
First, \textbf{prototype a fully working kernel before any deeper integration into
the engine}, so that the design is chosen empirically rather than frozen in
advance. Second, \textbf{conversion is the danger line}: the kernel's
$\beta\eta\delta\iota$ conversion must be its own small, decidable, resource-bounded
\MeTTa{} functions and must never be delegated to \CeTTa{}'s general runtime
rewriting, or proof checking ceases to be a terminating judgment. We present the
two reference kernels we mirror (the Lean \code{PureKernel} for \DTT{} and
Megalodon's \code{tp/tm/pf}+\code{check\_doc} core for \HOTG{}), a graded
\emph{curriculum} of the easiest examples that pin down the smallest fully
expressive kernel (a \DTT{} ladder following Smolka \& Brown's \emph{Introduction
to Computational Logic}, and an \HOTG{} ladder following Megalodon), and a
prototype-first build plan whose first milestone is an honest \emph{capacity ledger}
(\textsc{Agree}/\textsc{Diverge}/\textsc{Open}) rather than a claim of finality. The
\MeTTa{} kernel's verdicts are treated as a \emph{subject} to be measured against
the oracles, not as an oracle itself.
\end{abstract}

\section{Why a single kernel for two foundations}

Lean/Coq rest on the Calculus of Inductive Constructions: dependent function and
pair types, an inductive-definition mechanism with computation rules, a universe
hierarchy, and a \emph{decidable} definitional equality. Megalodon rests on
\HOTG{}: simple types with an impredicative \code{Prop}, proof terms, classical
logic, set membership, extensionality, foundational ($\in$-)induction, and a choice
operator. These are different mathematics, but a \emph{proof kernel} for either is
small: it is a term/type/proof object language plus a conversion relation plus a
checking judgment, with everything else (parsing, automation, optimization, the
ambient runtime) living \emph{outside} the trusted core.

Our thesis --- developed at length in the companion memo
\emph{metta\_proof\_kernel\_grounded} --- is that \MeTTa{} is an apt host for such a
kernel \emph{as a program}: terms, types, and proofs are \MeTTa{} atoms; the
checking and conversion judgments are \MeTTa{} functions; theories and imports are
\MeTTa{} data. The trusted waist is then a small auditable \MeTTa{} module, and the
two foundations appear as two \emph{checkers} sharing that waist. We are not
proposing to encode both foundations into one ambient rewriting theory (the
Dedukti route); we keep the foundations' judgments first-class and only share the
machinery beneath them.

\paragraph{What this document is.} A design + curriculum memo to drive a
prototype-first build. It records: the reference kernels we mirror
(\S\ref{sec:oracles}); the \MeTTa-program architecture (\S\ref{sec:arch}); the
graded curriculum that defines ``smallest fully expressive'' (\S\ref{sec:curric});
the build plan and its first milestone, a capacity ledger (\S\ref{sec:plan});
the conversion discipline that is the central hazard (\S\ref{sec:conv}); and the
open questions we deliberately leave open (\S\ref{sec:open}).

\section{The two reference kernels (our oracles and templates)}
\label{sec:oracles}

\subsection{\DTT{}: the Lean \code{PureKernel}}
A trusted, \emph{sorry-free} dependently-typed kernel already exists in Lean
(\code{Mettapedia/.../MeTTa/PureKernel/}, 28 files). Its term language is
\begin{lstlisting}
PureTm n ::= var (Fin n) | const DeclName | U0 | U1
           | pi A B | sigma A B | id A t u
           | lam B | app f a | pair t u | fst p | snd p | refl t
\end{lstlisting}
with two universes ($\code{U0}:\code{U1}$), $\Pi$, $\Sigma$, and identity types. It
provides one-step $\beta/\delta/\iota$ reduction, Church--Rosser confluence,
\code{HasType}, a \emph{decidable} definitional equality
(\code{defEqByNormalization?}), subject reduction, and a bidirectional checker.
Ordinary families (\code{Bool}, \code{Nat}, \code{Unit}) are \emph{not} hard-coded:
they enter through a general declaration mechanism (\code{DeclSpec},
\code{InductiveDecl}, \code{RecursorDecl}) with full well-formedness proofs. This is
both our \DTT{} \emph{oracle} and our design template; crucially, the existing
runtime-to-kernel bridge marks \code{directExecEquivalence} as \code{false}, so the
runtime is \emph{not} yet known to agree with this kernel.

\subsection{\HOTG{}: the Megalodon \code{mathdata} core}
Megalodon's trusted core (\code{repos/megalodon-1.13/src/mathdata.mli}) is genuinely
compact --- three small inductive types and a checker:
\begin{lstlisting}
tp ::= TpVar i | Prop | Base i | Ar(tp,tp) | TpAll tp        (* simple types + type-vars + type-forall *)
tm ::= DB i | TmH h | Prim i | Ap(tm,tm) | Lam(tp,tm)
     | Imp(tm,tm) | All(tp,tm) | TTpAp(tm,tp) | TTpLam tm | TTpAll tm
pf ::= Gpa h | Hyp i | Known h | PTmAp(pf,tm) | PPfAp(pf,pf)
     | PLam(tm,pf) | TLam(tp,pf) | PTpAp(pf,tp) | PTpLam pf
\end{lstlisting}
plus \code{check\_doc} (the proof checker), $\beta\eta\delta$ normalization with
resource limits (\code{BetaLimit}, \code{NonNormalTerm}), and hash-addressed
\code{doc}/\code{theory}/\code{signa} imports. Logic and set-theoretic content are
\emph{not} in this core: \code{True}/\code{False}/\code{not}/\code{and}/\code{or}
are impredicative definitions, and membership/extensionality/choice/$\in$-induction
enter as \emph{axioms} in a preamble (Egal mode uses \code{Eps} choice and Tarski's
axiom; Mizar mode uses \code{the} and ZF-style axioms). The seed file
\code{Mettapedia/megalodon/experiments/01\_basics.mg} proves eight propositional
theorems entirely by proof-term lambdas (e.g.\
\code{andEL := fun A B H => H A (fun a b => a)}).

\subsection{Precedent that \MeTTa{} can host a checker}
\code{hyperon/metamath/pverify} (with \code{lib/pverify-utils.metta}) is a Metamath
proof checker realized as a \MeTTa{} program. It establishes feasibility of the
\emph{host}; it is Metamath, not \HOTG{}, so it is precedent, not evidence about the
present design.

\section{Architecture: the kernel as a \MeTTa{} program}
\label{sec:arch}

\paragraph{Shared waist (written once in \MeTTa{}).} Atom encodings for binders
(de Bruijn or locally nameless), $\alpha$-safe substitution, typed contexts,
declarations (parameter / definition / axiom / theorem / inductive / import), proof
objects, the \textbf{conversion engine} ($\beta/\eta/\delta/\iota$ as explicit,
terminating, resource-capped \MeTTa{} functions), a \code{check}/\code{infer}
driver, and hashing/provenance for imports. The two checkers below are
\emph{instances} of this waist --- that sharing \emph{is} the early unification.

\paragraph{\HOTG{} checker.} Mirror \code{tp}/\code{tm}/\code{pf} + \code{check\_doc}
+ $\beta\eta\delta$ as \MeTTa{} atoms and functions. Proof-term checking over
impredicative \code{Prop}; classical/set/TG axioms are a \emph{profile} layered
above the core, admitted only after the bare proof-term checker is green.

\paragraph{\DTT{} checker.} Mirror \code{PureTm} ($\code{U0}/\code{U1}$, $\Pi$,
$\Sigma$, \code{Id}) with bidirectional infer/check, $\beta/\delta/\iota$, and
decidable definitional equality by normalization; inductive families via
\code{DeclSpec}, never hard-coded. Where the \CeTTa{} engine's \code{--profile
he-prime} already provides dependent type inference, the \MeTTa{} kernel may use it;
new C features are added to \code{he-prime} \emph{only} when the prototype proves a
concrete need.

\section{Curriculum: the examples that pin the smallest fully expressive kernel}
\label{sec:curric}

The curriculum \emph{defines} ``fully expressive'': the smallest kernel is the one
that checks every example below. The \DTT{} ladder follows the chapter order of
Smolka \& Brown's \emph{Introduction to Computational Logic} (a Coq course by
Megalodon's own author), which introduces kernel features in increasing dependency
order. The \HOTG{} ladder follows Megalodon.

\paragraph{L0 --- shared waist (both ladders ride on it).} Names/binding,
$\alpha$-safe substitution, typed contexts, declarations, proof objects,
conversion ($\beta/\eta/\delta/\iota$, decidable), hash imports + provenance,
negative tests.

\paragraph{Cross-profile smoke.} $P\to P$;\ $P\to Q\to P$;\ $P\wedge Q\to P$;\
$\bot\to P$;\ equality reflexivity/congruence;\ pair projections;\ and \emph{one}
tiny \CeTTa{} operational-semantics theorem treated as a \emph{certificate}, never
as definitional equality.

\paragraph{Negative corpus (a fail-by-default gate --- the soundness backstop).}
Bad de Bruijn index;\ unknown \code{Known}/\code{Hyp};\ application type mismatch;\
a proposition-shape used as a proof object;\ cyclic/non-normal $\delta$;\
\textbf{an arbitrary \MeTTa{} rewrite admitted as conversion} (the central danger);\
stale import/hash;\ \code{VecN \dots -1} wrongly accepted. The kernel must
\emph{reject} every item here; a checker that accepts any of them is unsound.

\section{Prototype-first build plan}
\label{sec:plan}

\begin{description}
\item[Phase 0.] Shared \MeTTa{} waist + the ledger harness (three deciders:
  \MeTTa{}-kernel vs.\ Lean \code{PureKernel} oracle vs.\ \code{he-prime} C).
\item[Phase 1 (\HOTG{} prototype).] \MeTTa{} \code{tp/tm/pf} + proof-term checker +
  $\beta$; \emph{check the eight \code{01\_basics.mg} theorems green} (H1--H2).
\item[Phase 2 (\DTT{} prototype).] \MeTTa{} \code{PureTm} + bidirectional checker +
  $\beta/\delta/\iota$ + decidable def-eq; \emph{D1--D5 green}; ledger vs.\ the Lean
  oracle and \code{he-prime} C.
\item[Phase 3 (unify + harden).] Both checkers share the waist; cross-profile smoke
  green; \emph{negative corpus rejected}.
\item[Phase 4 (climb the ladders).] \HOTG{} H3--H5 (definitions, equality, then
  set/TG axioms); \DTT{} D6--D9 (lists, \code{Vec}, inductive predicates,
  recursors/universes); resolve the \code{VecN} index-domain question.
\item[Later.] Only after a fully working prototype and its lessons: consider deeper
  substrate integration --- add the \code{he-prime} C features the prototype proved
  necessary; refine \CeTTa{}. Not before.
\end{description}

\paragraph{Milestone 1 (the ledger, not a constitution).} The deliverable is the
working H1 + D1--D3 \MeTTa{} kernel plus a populated \emph{capacity ledger} with
buckets \textsc{Agree-Accept}, \textsc{Agree-Reject}, \textsc{Diverge},
\textsc{Open}, plus the negative-corpus rejections. Divergences and open cases are
first-class outputs, not blemishes to hide. Only then does external review become
useful, and its question is whether the fragment boundary is honest and which
divergences are bugs versus legitimate language semantics --- not whether the final
kernel is settled. The \MeTTa{}-kernel verdicts are labelled \textbf{subject}; the
Lean \code{PureKernel} and Megalodon are the \textbf{oracles}.

\section{Conversion discipline (the central hazard)}
\label{sec:conv}
The single biggest architectural risk is identifying kernel conversion with general
\MeTTa{} evaluation. We hold five lines:
(A) decidable conversion $\ne$ general \MeTTa{} eval --- the kernel's
$\beta/\eta/\delta/\iota$ are its own bounded \MeTTa{} functions (resource caps in
the spirit of Megalodon's \code{BetaLimit});
(B) \MeTTa{}'s many-valued/nondeterministic typing vs.\ a functional \DTT{}
judgment --- the kernel commits to a functional infer/check;
(C) grounded/FFI values are a trusted boundary --- grounded equality is not kernel
reduction;
(D) universe consistency is not optional forever, because proofs \emph{about}
\MeTTa{} are a goal;
(E) the kernel must be small and separable --- a pure checking slice, not entangled
in effectful evaluation.

\section{Open questions (kept deliberately open)}
\label{sec:open}
(1) Should \code{VecN String -1} be accepted under \code{Number} indexing, or should
tranche~1 restrict indexed families to \code{Nat}? (2) How much definitional
equality belongs in the first tranche? (3) What stays in the trusted runtime core
versus later self-hosted \MeTTa{} rules? (4) What fragment of dependent typing is
promised first? (5) Does the \code{PureKernel}-style core remain the convergence
target for the runtime-facing tranche, given \code{directExecEquivalence = false}?
